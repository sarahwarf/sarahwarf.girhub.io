About

I am a language educator and teacher trainer specializing in the integration of AI into university language teaching. I currently teach academic English at NYU Shanghai and am completing a PhD in Education at Pädagogische Hochschule Heidelberg (Germany), where my research examines how university faculty understand and adopt AI in their teaching. My work explores practical ways educators can use AI tools to support language learning, feedback, and assessment while maintaining human-centered classroom practices. Through teaching, research, and professional development workshops, I focus on helping instructors experiment with new technologies in ways that strengthen pedagogy rather than replace it.

Experience and Focus

  • Senior Lecturer in Academic English at NYU Shanghai since 2019

  • Regular invited speaker and presenter at international conferences on English language teaching, higher education, and AI in education

Faculty Development Workshops

I offer workshops and invited talks for university teachers that can be delivered as individual sessions, short workshop series, or full professional development programs, either online or in person. These sessions are designed for instructors working in EFL, EMI, and academic English contexts and focus on practical strategies teachers can apply directly in their classrooms. My sessions generally fall into two broad areas: AI-integrated teaching and language pedagogy. Universities and departments often request a specific topic, and sessions can be adapted to local needs.

AI-Integrated Teaching

  • Humanistic approaches to AI in the language classroom
  • AI tools for speaking practice and oral feedback
  • Generative AI and language assessment
  • Chatbots and conversational AI in language learning
  • AI literacy for teachers and students
  • Designing AI-integrated classroom tasks
  • Ethical questions: academic integrity, authorship, and AI use
  • Practical AI tools for materials design and lesson planning

Language Teaching & Pedagogy

  • Teaching speaking and interactional skills in L2 classrooms
  • Fluency-building tasks for language learners
  • Teaching paraphrasing, synthesis, and academic integrity
  • Designing effective academic reading tasks
  • Graphic organizers and scaffolding for academic literacy
  • Formative assessment strategies for large language classes
  • Teaching critical thinking in EFL contexts
  • Integrating listening into reading-focused courses
  • Building student engagement and participation in language classrooms
